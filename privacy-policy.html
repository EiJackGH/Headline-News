Reader Privacy Policy & Data Disclosure

Published by EiJackGH Media Operations • Transparency Guidelines for Site Visitors & Subscribers
Privacy Commitment: Headline News prioritizes user privacy. We do not sell reader data, utilize invasive tracking cookies, or build personal profiles on our audience members.
1. Data Collection 2. Hosting & Analytics 3. Cookies & Local Storage 4. External Links 5. Your Privacy Rights

1. Information We Collect

Headline News operates on a privacy-first static delivery architecture. We minimize data intake to only what is strictly necessary to render publication content efficiently:

  • Log Data: Server requests automatically collect standard diagnostic metadata such as your IP address, browser type, operating system, referring URL, and time of access.
  • User Inquiries: If you interact with our public repository via GitHub Issues or pull requests, your public profile handle and submitted text will be visible per GitHub standards.

2. Infrastructure & Hosting (GitHub Pages)

This digital publication is statically hosted on GitHub Pages. As part of providing web traffic delivery and security mitigations (e.g., DDoS protection), GitHub may collect basic network log information from site visitors.

For more details regarding GitHub's infrastructure telemetry, please refer to the GitHub Privacy Statement.

3. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

Headline News does not use cross-site tracking cookies, third-party advertising trackers, or fingerprinting scripts. Any local browser storage utilized by this site is reserved strictly for site performance (such as preserving light or dark mode preferences).

4. Third-Party Links & External Reporting

Our news coverage and editorial posts may contain hyperlinks to external media outlets, primary sources, or research archives. Headline News is not responsible for the privacy practices, content, or security of external websites once you navigate away from our domain.

5. Data Protection Rights

Because we do not store personal profiles or track readers across the web, we do not maintain invasive personal datasets. However, under global privacy frameworks (including GDPR and CCPA), readers retain the right to request clarification regarding any public contributions or repository records.
